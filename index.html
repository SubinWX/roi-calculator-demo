How The Savings Are Calculated

Step 1 — Daily Wasted Cost

Formula:

Daily Wasted Cost = (# employees) × (1.8 hours) × (hourly wage)

Example:

  • 50 employees
  • $40/hour wage
  • 1.8 hours wasted per day
50 × 1.8 × 40 = 3,600

Daily Wasted Cost = $3,600/day

Meaning: The company loses about $3,600 every day because employees spend time searching for documents and information.

Step 2 — Monthly Savings

M-Files improves search efficiency by 40%.

So the tool assumes:

40% of that wasted cost can be recovered

Formula:

Monthly Savings = (Daily Wasted Cost × 20 days) × 0.40

Calculation:

3,600 × 20 = 72,000

72,000 × 0.40 = 28,800

Estimated Monthly Savings = $28,800

Step 3 — Annual Savings

Formula:

Annual Savings = (Daily Wasted Cost × 240 days) × 0.40

Calculation:

3,600 × 240 = 864,000

864,000 × 0.40 = 345,600

Estimated Annual Savings = $345,600

So the “hours” are not missing — they are already converted into money during the Daily Wasted Cost calculation by multiplying:

hours wasted × hourly wage

That converts lost time into lost money.

Note: Concurrent projects are captured for enrichment only and do not affect the savings calculation.
